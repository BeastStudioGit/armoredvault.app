Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the questions I hear most.

How do I create a strong passphrase?

Use the built-in Generate Passphrase tool in Settings. It rolls true random dice against the EFF Large Wordlist to build a Diceware passphrase of 8, 10, or 12 words. A 10-word Diceware passphrase is ~129 bits of entropy — roughly 25.6 quintillion years to crack at one trillion guesses per second. You can also enter your own passphrase; a live strength meter shows the estimated crack time as you type.

What happens if I forget my passphrase?

AES-256 encryption cannot be reversed without your passphrase — your files cannot be decrypted by me, by Apple, or by anyone else. However, the app includes a Vault Reset Zone: tap "Forgot passphrase?" on the lock screen, authenticate with Face ID / Touch ID / device passcode, type RESET, and the current vault mode is wiped so you can start fresh with a new passphrase. This is a clean-slate reset, not a decryption back door — your encrypted files in that vault mode are permanently erased. Launch codes, app settings, the Intruder Log, and the other vault mode are preserved.

Does the app send my data anywhere?

No. Armored Vault works entirely offline. No data ever leaves your device unless you explicitly export it. There are no analytics, no ad networks, no third-party SDKs, and no outbound network calls.

What encryption does the app use?

AES-256-GCM — the same standard used by governments and financial institutions. Files are encrypted the moment they enter the vault, using a key derived from your passphrase via Argon2id. Decryption happens in a streaming chunked pipeline so large videos play back instantly without a pre-decrypt wait.

Can I back up my vault?

Yes. Use Full Backup in Settings to export your entire vault to a USB-C external drive as flat decrypted files — no staging, straight to the drive. You can also use Export Encrypted for an AES-256 password-protected ZIP that opens in 7-Zip / WinZip on any computer.

What external drives are supported?

Any USB-C external SSD. For best results, format the drive as APFS on a Mac before use — APFS supports the full Unicode filename set, recovers cleanly from interrupted writes, and outperforms FAT-family filesystems on large videos.

What iPad models are supported?

Any iPad running iPadOS 18 or later. Universal Purchase is disabled — this is an iPad-only app, not a scaled phone app.
