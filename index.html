Security architecture

Defence in depth. Nothing is stored in plain text. Ever.

Per-file keys & Secure Enclave

A unique AES-256 key is generated for every file. The master key is derived from your passphrase and stored in the iOS Keychain, protected by the device's Secure Enclave.

Zero network access

The app makes no outbound connections of any kind. No analytics. No telemetry. No cloud sync. No accounts. Nothing leaves your device unless you explicitly export it.

Chunked streaming decryption

Videos are decrypted chunk-by-chunk on demand during playback. No temporary plaintext file ever hits the filesystem. Plaintext chunks are zeroed from memory after use.

App lifecycle protection

Privacy screen blacks out in the app switcher. Auto-lock on background. Face-down detection. Kill switch wipes keys from memory on any screen.

Brute-force lockout

Exponential back-off after three failed attempts — 5 seconds, then doubling, capped at 5 minutes. Lockout state survives app restarts.

Backup exclusion

Encrypted files are excluded from iCloud and iTunes backups. Keys are device-bound — restoring a backup to a new device will not recover the vault.

Frequently asked questions

How do I create a strong passphrase?

Use the built-in Generate Passphrase tool in Settings. It rolls true random dice against the EFF Large Wordlist to build a Diceware passphrase of 8, 10, or 12 words. A 10-word passphrase is ~129 bits — roughly 25.6 quintillion years to crack at a trillion guesses per second. You can also enter your own passphrase; a live strength meter rates it on the spot.

What happens if I forget my passphrase?

AES-256 encryption cannot be reversed without your passphrase — your files cannot be decrypted by me, by Apple, or by anyone else. The app includes a Vault Reset Zone: tap "Forgot passphrase?" on the lock screen, authenticate with Face ID / Touch ID / device passcode, type RESET, and the current vault mode is wiped so you can start fresh. This is a clean-slate reset, not a decryption back door — your old encrypted files are permanently erased. Launch codes, app settings, the Intruder Log, and the other vault mode are preserved.

Does the app send my data anywhere?

No. Armored Vault makes zero network connections of any kind. No analytics, no telemetry, no cloud, no accounts. Nothing leaves your device unless you explicitly export it.

If there's no cloud, why can I import from iCloud?

Armored Vault uses iOS's native Files picker for imports, so it can pull from any source iOS exposes — On My iPad, iCloud Drive, Dropbox, Google Drive, or anything else you've connected. The import is strictly one-way: files are copied in, encrypted on arrival, and from that moment live only on this device. The app itself still makes zero network calls and has no account, no sync, and no link back to iCloud or any other provider. iCloud is a source you pull from, not a cloud the app lives in.

What encryption does the app use?

AES-256-GCM — the same authenticated-encryption standard used by governments and financial institutions. Files are encrypted the moment they enter the vault. The master key is stored in the iOS Keychain, protected by the device's Secure Enclave.

Can I back up my vault?

Yes — three ways. Export Encrypted creates a password-protected AES-256 ZIP. Export Decrypted to ZIP creates a standard ZIP for sharing. Full Backup writes flat decrypted files straight to a USB-C external drive with no staging on your iPad.

What external drives are supported?

Any USB-C external SSD. For best results, format the drive as APFS on a Mac before use. APFS supports the full Unicode filename set, recovers cleanly from interrupted writes, and outperforms FAT-family filesystems on large videos.

Does the app work without internet?

Yes — entirely. The app has no online dependencies. It also has no network permissions.

What iPad models are supported?

Any iPad running iPadOS 18 or later. Universal Purchase is disabled — this is an iPad-only app, not a scaled phone app.
